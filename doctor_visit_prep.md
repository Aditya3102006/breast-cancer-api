# Clinical Knowledge Base: Preparing for Doctor Consultations

## Introduction
Receiving a breast cancer diagnosis is overwhelming. Facing multiple specialists can make it difficult for patients to remember what to ask. Preparing a list of questions in advance and bringing a trusted friend or family member to take notes is highly recommended.

---

## 1. Questions to Ask the Pathologist or Diagnosing Doctor
- What type of breast cancer do I have (e.g., ductal or lobular, invasive or in situ)?
- Is my cancer hormone receptor-positive (ER+/PR+) or HER2-positive?
- What is the grade of the tumor, and what does it mean for my prognosis?
- Has the cancer spread to my lymph nodes or surrounding blood/lymph vessels?
- Do I need any genetic testing (e.g., BRCA1/BRCA2 mutations)?

---

## 2. Questions to Ask the Breast Surgeon
- Am I a candidate for breast-conserving surgery (lumpectomy), or do I need a mastectomy? Why?
- Do you recommend removing lymph nodes (Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy vs. Axillary Dissection)?
- What are the risks of lymphedema, and how can I prevent it?
- Can I have breast reconstruction at the same time as my mastectomy, or does it need to be delayed?
- What will my recovery look like, and how long will I be in the hospital?
- Will I have surgical drains, and how do I care for them at home?

---

## 3. Questions to Ask the Medical Oncologist
- Will I need chemotherapy, hormone therapy, targeted therapy, or immunotherapy?
- What is the goal of these treatments (e.g., to shrink the tumor before surgery, or to prevent recurrence after surgery)?
- When will my treatment start, and how long will the entire course last?
- What drugs will I receive, and how are they administered (e.g., IV port, pills)?
- What are the short-term and long-term side effects of these drugs (e.g., neuropathy, heart risks, fertility issues)?
- If I want to preserve my fertility, what are my options before starting treatment?
- How will we monitor if the treatment is working?

---

## 4. Questions to Ask the Radiation Oncologist
- Do I need radiation therapy? Why?
- When should radiation start, and how many weeks will it last?
- What are the potential side effects of radiation on my skin, lung, or heart (especially for left-sided breast cancers)?
- Can I continue my normal activities or work during radiation treatments?
- How often will I have follow-up visits with you after treatment ends?

---

## 5. Practical Checklist for the Patient
1. **Request Copies**: Always ask for physical or digital copies of your pathology reports, imaging scans (mammograms, ultrasounds, MRIs on CD), and lab results.
2. **Bring a Notebook**: Write down key terms the doctor uses. Ask them to clarify any medical jargon.
3. **Record the Session**: Ask your doctor's permission to record the audio of the consultation on your phone so you can listen to it again at home with family.
4. **Clarify Contacts**: Write down the contact information of the clinical nurse navigator or triage line to call if you have questions between appointments.
